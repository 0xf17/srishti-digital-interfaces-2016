Kudankulam Nuclear Power Plant is a nuclear power station in Koodankulam in the Tirunelveli district of the southern Indian state of Tamil Nadu. Construction on the plant began on 31 March 2002, but faced several delays due to the fishermen's objection.During the two and a half years of its

operation, the first reactor at the Kudankulam Nuclear Power Plant (KKNPP-1), built jointly by the Nuclear Power Corporation of India Ltd (NPCIL) and the Russian company Atomstroyexport (ASE) has not reported any serious accident.This first and only operating pressurized

water reactor (VVER-1000) belonging to Generation-III is advertised as the safest reactor in the world. There are plans to build four more reactors of similar vintage at the same site located on the coast of the Bay of Bengal in the Tamil Nadu state of India.
